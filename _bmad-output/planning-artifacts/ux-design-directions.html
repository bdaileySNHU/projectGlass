Design Direction Exploration

Three variations on the same core vision — a Tokyo Night dark gallery. The differences are intentionally subtle: density, header treatment, hover behavior, and EXIF presentation.

Direction A

Tight & Lively

12px gaps, centered header, scale-on-hover. The grid feels dense and energetic — lots of photos visible at once. Hover scale creates a sense of life and interactivity.

Direction B

Spacious & Subtle

20px gaps, left-aligned header with subtitle, brightness hover. More breathing room between photos. The slight dimming of non-hovered photos creates a quiet spotlight effect.

Direction C

Ultra-Minimal

8px gaps, uppercase micro-header, no hover effect. Maximum photo density. The grid feels like a seamless mosaic. The UI is almost completely absent — just photographs.

Design Direction Summary

Side-by-side comparison of all variations. Mix and match elements from different directions.

Element Direction A: Tight Direction B: Spacious Direction C: Minimal

Header Centered, 18px light Left-aligned + subtitle Centered, 14px uppercase
Hover FX Scale 1.03 Brightness lift None
Feeling Dense, energetic, interactive Airy, refined, curated Seamless, silent, maximum photo
Best For Large collections, engagement Curated portfolios, craft Letting photos speak entirely
Sally's Recommendation
Direction A (Tight) as the base with Lightbox Option 1 (Below). The 12px gap and scale hover best serve "the grid is the product" — dense enough to show breadth, interactive enough to feel alive. The centered header keeps things symmetric and clean. EXIF below the photo is the simplest, most responsive approach — works identically on all devices with no layout gymnastics.
